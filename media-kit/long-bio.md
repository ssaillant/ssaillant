# Long Bio

Said Saillant, Ph.D., works on AI governance, AI safety, frontier institutions, industrial strategy, and the design of credible public-interest frameworks for advanced AI.

His recent work has focused on AI-ready special economic zones, frontier governance, middle-power diplomacy, Global South strategy for advanced AI, and institutional design that can survive contact with politics. He has contributed to AI policy and legislative design, developed research on institutional models for technology-led development, and participated in international discussions on AI standards, governance, diplomacy, and the long-run societal implications of advanced AI.

Said holds a Ph.D. in Philosophy from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), an M.B.A. from Quantic School of Business and Technology, and a B.A. summa cum laude from Rutgers University in Philosophy, Psychology, and Cognitive Science.

His work combines philosophical analysis, policy reasoning, strategic judgment, and technical literacy. He speaks and advises on AI governance, AI safety, frontier governance, AI-ready special economic zones, industrial strategy, diplomacy, and the philosophy of advanced AI.
